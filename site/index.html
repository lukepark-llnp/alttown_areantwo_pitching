01 — The Opportunity

The whitepaper promises it.
The platform must deliver it.

The $ATWO whitepaper defines six product modules — Watch, Vote, Stake, Earn, Teams, Marketplace — and a franchise model where fan staking funds club treasuries. What it doesn't yet have is the exchange layer that turns 7 franchises into 7 living fan economies. That layer already exists, live in production: Alt.town.

WHITEPAPER §5 — VOTE & STAKE

Utility needs a market

Voting and staking create demand for tokens — but fans also need price discovery, liquidity and an exit. A per-club order book turns engagement into a real economy, not a one-way sink.

WHITEPAPER §11 — FRANCHISE MODEL

7 clubs, 7 economies

Presidents with 15–60M followers each. Every franchise deserves its own token, its own treasury and its own fandom market — under one $ATWO reserve, on one platform.

Arena Two already sells $99 memberships via Stripe. Alt.town's credit system (email sign-up, custodial wallets, pegged in-app credits) lets any football fan trade club tokens without ever touching a DEX.

DNA Engine

Each IP issues 1M fungible tokens with programmatic allocation, lockups and vesting (30% IP / 10% presale / 20% community / 40% ecosystem). Battle-tested with live virtual-celebrity IPs — the exact machinery a sports franchise token needs.

3-Round Presale + Order Book

Fixed-price presale (50% off → 30% off → base price) that converts into open limit/market-order trading. 70%+ of presale revenue settles to the IP — for Arena Two, straight into franchise treasuries.

Web 2.5 Credit System

A pegged in-app credit (KEY) minted and burned against the platform token, plus free reward points (Mile) earned from engagement. Email sign-up, custodial wallets, fiat-friendly. Fans trade like it's a fantasy app; settlement is fully on-chain.

Governance Stack

Staking → ve-token → on-chain proposals, including per-IP governance where token holders vote on ecosystem unlocks and fee changes. Maps 1:1 onto the whitepaper's "Governance & Protocol Upgrades" pillar.

Migration Experience

The team has already executed a full smart-contract migration and a token reallocation in production. Porting the stack from BNB/opBNB to Base is routine EVM work — the whitepaper itself cites full EVM compatibility as a reason Base was chosen.

Korea & Asia Playbook

A Seoul-based product team fluent in K-fandom economics — the same fandom that powers Dokkaebi FC (Park Ji-sung + Xiumin) and the Seoul matchday at KSPO Dome, plus the Kuala Lumpur and Singapore legs of the tour.
